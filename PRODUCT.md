# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local drivers and vehicle owners in Hockenheim and the surrounding area (Schwetzingen, Mannheim, Heidelberg, Speyer) who need car repair, an oil change/inspection, HU/AU (TÜV) prep, or want to buy/sell a used vehicle. They typically arrive via local search, are comparing workshops, and care about price transparency and getting an appointment quickly. The primary conversion action is a phone call (email as fallback); the FAQ and schema.org markup also support local SEO and rich snippets.

## Product Purpose

Single-page marketing site for KFZ-Service01, an independent Autowerkstatt in Hockenheim with 40+ years of experience. It builds trust (transparent pricing, fast scheduling, all makes/models serviced, free Kostenvoranschlag), presents services (Leistungen), opening hours and location, and drives phone calls and email inquiries. Success means more calls/Termine and stronger local search visibility. The site is live at kfz-service01.de, deployed via Vercel.

## Brand Personality

**Bodenständig & vertrauenswürdig** (down-to-earth, trustworthy). Family-workshop feel: honest, no hidden costs, personal and approachable rather than aspirational or corporate. Confident from 40+ years of experience, but warm, not flashy or "techy for its own sake."

## Anti-references

- **Generische Ketten-Werkstätten** — sterile corporate chain sites (ATU/Pitstop-style blue corporate look).
- **Glossy Autohaus-Sites** — luxury car dealer aesthetics; too aspirational/expensive-feeling for everyday repair customers.
- **Veraltete Kleinbetrieb-Sites** — dated 2005-era local-business sites (clipart, Comic Sans, cluttered).

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through transparency** — pricing honesty and "kostenloser Kostenvoranschlag" are the trust anchor; keep them visible and prominent, never buried.
2. **Local and personal, not corporate** — warm tones, direct "Sie" address, human language over institutional phrasing; avoid sterile blue-corporate patterns.
3. **One tap from a call** — the phone CTA must stay reachable from anywhere on the page with minimal friction.
4. **Skilled but warm** — the gear/technical motifs should read as craft and precision, not cold or sterile; pair technical details with approachable copy.
5. **Readable for an older driver demographic** — favor higher contrast and slightly larger body text over the strict AA minimum; avoid small light-gray type.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline, with a deliberate lean toward higher contrast and larger body text given the likely older customer demographic for a car repair shop. `prefers-reduced-motion` is already respected in the hero gear animation, scroll-reveal, and morph blob; preserve this in any future motion work.
